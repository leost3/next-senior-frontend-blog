# ADR-001: Content-First Blog Over Portfolio Hybrid or Interactive Playground

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-10

## Context

Leonardo is a senior frontend developer building a personal blog to support career growth — specifically to become a conversation starter in technical interviews. The primary audience is recruiters, hiring managers, and peer developers. The blog posts irregularly and the core success metric is posts being referenced by interviewers, not volume metrics or project showcase breadth.

Three structural approaches were evaluated: centering the product on written content, splitting it between projects and writing, or making every post an interactive live demo.

## Decision

Adopt a content-first blog structure. Blog posts are the primary product. The home page surfaces recent posts, a brief identity statement, and contact links (LinkedIn, email, CV download). The blog itself serves as a live proof of craft: its own Core Web Vitals are displayed as a widget on the page. Technical complexity (edge search, optimistic likes, real user metrics) is woven into the infrastructure, not the content format.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Portfolio + Blog Hybrid

- **Description**: Home page split between featured project case studies (with screenshots and outcomes) and a writing section
- **Pros**: More complete picture for recruiters; demonstrates breadth of past work
- **Cons**: Higher scope; two content types require two maintenance paths; dilutes the "blog as proof" message; irregular poster may leave the project section sparse and unconvincing
- **Why rejected**: The goal is interview conversations about technical thinking — writing that shows decisions and tradeoffs achieves this better than a project grid. Project showcase deferred to Phase 2.

### Alternative 2: Interactive Playground Blog

- **Description**: Every post embeds live runnable demos of the concepts it covers (before/after toggles, sandboxed components)
- **Pros**: Maximum technical impression; demonstrates implementation skill visibly
- **Cons**: Very high authoring effort per post; unsustainable for an irregular publisher; high risk of the blog remaining empty or stale
- **Why rejected**: Authoring friction kills the content pipeline. The blog being well-built already demonstrates implementation skill without requiring every post to include a live demo.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Lower authoring overhead per post — lowers the bar to publish
- The "blog as live demo" (visible perf metrics) provides technical proof without requiring interactive embeds
- Clean separation of concerns: infrastructure complexity lives in the platform, not in each post

### Negative

- No explicit project showcase in Phase 1 — recruiters who want to see past work must rely on posts or external links
- Success depends on post quality; a sparse or shallow writing history weakens the signal

### Risks

- Risk: Irregular posting leaves the blog feeling abandoned. Mitigation: Phase 1 ships with 2–3 seed posts so the blog is never empty at launch.
